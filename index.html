What is the reluctance?
Reluctance is a measurement of the resistance of a closed magnetic circuit to a magnetic flux, equal to the ratio of the magnetomotive force to the magnetic flux.
How to calculate reluctance?
To calculate reluctance you should use the following formula:
R = F / Φ
where:
R = reluctance in ampere-turns per weber
F = magnetomotive force
Φ = magnetic flux in webers
Why use reluctance?
Reluctance motors is sued in order to deliver very high power density at low cost.
